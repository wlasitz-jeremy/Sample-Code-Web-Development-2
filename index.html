About the Film

Released in 1970, The Aristocats is the 20th animated feature film produced by Walt Disney Productions. Set in Paris in 1910, the story follows a family of aristocratic cats — Duchess and her three kittens — who are catnapped by the scheming butler Edgar and must find their way home with the help of the alley cat Thomas O'Malley.

The film was the last to be approved by Walt Disney himself before his death in 1966, and features a beloved jazz-infused soundtrack that has endured for generations.

The Characters

🐈

Duchess

The elegant white mother cat and prized pet of Madame Adelaide Bonfamille. Graceful, kind, and fiercely devoted to her kittens.

🧡

Marie

The only daughter of Duchess. Prim, proper, and convinced that ladies do not start fights — they finish them.

🎨

Toulouse

The eldest kitten with a passion for painting. He idolises Thomas O'Malley and wants to grow up to be just like him.

🎹

Berlioz

The youngest kitten and the musical one of the litter. Shy but talented, often found at the piano.

🧣

Thomas O'Malley

A street-smart, free-spirited alley cat who goes by the full name Abraham de Lacy Giuseppe Casey Thomas O'Malley. Roguishly charming.

🎷

Scat Cat

O'Malley's old friend and leader of an eclectic jazz band of alley cats from around the world. The life of every party.

"Everybody wants to be a cat, because a cat's the only cat who knows where it's at."

— Scat Cat & the Alley Cats

Fun Facts

  • The film was originally meant to be a two-part episode of the Wonderful World of Disney TV series.
  • Phil Harris, who voiced Baloo in The Jungle Book, also voiced Thomas O'Malley.
  • The alley cats in Scat Cat's band each represent a different country, including England, China, Russia, and Italy.
  • It was the first Disney animated film to use xerography for the entire production.
  • The film grossed $55 million on its original theatrical release.
